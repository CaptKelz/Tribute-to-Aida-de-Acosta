Aida de Acosta

First woman to fly a powered aircraft

Aida de Acosta © Bain News Service

Here is a summary of Aida de Acosta's life:

  • 1884 - Born in Longbranch, New Jersey
  • 1903 - In Paris, Alberto Santos-Dumont taught her how to fly "No. 9", his personal dirigible. She flew "No. 9" from Paris to Chateau de Bagatelle, where she watched Polo with Santos-Dumont. She then flew to Neuilly St. James. The entire trip lasted one and a half hours and earned her the honor of being the first woman to fly a powered aircraft solo.
  • 1908 - Married Oren Root II. They had two children, Oren (in 1911) and Alva (in 1914). They divorced in 1922.
  • 1922 - Afflicted with glaucoma, she lost sight in one eye and was inspired to organize a fund-raising campaign. It raised $3 million and funded the establishment of the Wilmer Eye Institure in 1925. This was the first eye institure in the U.S.
  • 1927 - Married Colonel Henry S. Breckinridge. They divorced in 1947.
  • 1945 - Became Executive Director of the Eye-Bank for Sight Restoration in New York.
  • 1962 - Died in Bedford, New York at the age of 81.

"Mademoiselle, vous êtes la première aero-chauffeuse du monde!"
(Miss, you are the first woman aero-driver in the world!) - Alberto Santos-Dumont
